How It Works

  • Email content is hashed with SHA-256
  • The hash is written to an XRP Ledger transaction memo
  • The transaction hash becomes your certified receipt
  • Anyone can verify by looking up the transaction on the public ledger

Why XRP?

Transactions settle in 3-5 seconds and cost fractions of a cent (typically 10-12 drops, or ~$0.00001). No mining, no waiting, no significant cost.

Quantum Security

SHA-256 content hashes are considered quantum-resistant. ECDSA signing keys used by XRP are theoretically vulnerable to Shor's algorithm on a sufficiently powerful quantum computer, but no such computer currently exists. The XRP Ledger community is exploring post-quantum signature schemes.

For more on post-quantum security research, visit the Quantum Security Lab.
